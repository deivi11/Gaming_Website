The 1980's brought the competitiveness between home computers and video game consoles.
People were not buying the idea of purchasing gaming consoles when they already had a
machine at home that could do the functions that this new machine was offering. During this
period, we had the Video Game crash in North America. This crash was the largest crash there
ever was. It was due to the oversaturated game console market and also the competition in
prices between the computer homes and the consoles started to increase price.
Many games such as Pacman on arcade were very successful so Atari decided to buy the rights
and included Pacman on their consoles and it was a huge failure due to the games resolution and
other factors such as loss of publishing rights due to many consoles being named and thrown at the public.
